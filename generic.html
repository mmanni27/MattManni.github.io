My Story

My name is Matt Manni, an aspiring data analyst with a diverse professional background. I am passionate about solving problems with data and helping people with solutions from data-driven insights. Although I do not have professional experience as a Data Analyst, I am excited to transition into this field as I embark on the next chapter of my life. My recently acquired Google Data Analytics Professional Certificate and continued learning have provided me with the necessary skills to achieve my goal.

A little bit about my background:

I was born and raised in Newport Beach, CA, spending most of my childhood and adult life in the area. I hold a Bachelor's degree in Geology (UC Santa Barbara) and a Master's degree in Spatial Information (University of New South Wales). Spatial information is information about the locations and shapes of geographic features and the relationships between them. This is typically referred to as GIS (Geographic Information System) and includes any data with a reference to a specific geographic location. I moved to Sydney, Australia for a year to obtain my Master's degree with the goal of using my knowledge of geology and spatial information for mineral exploration in Australia. I had chosen this topic for my research paper at UNSW, using satellite imagery of North West Queensland, Australia to locate potential mining opportunities in the area.

Unfortunately, that plan did not work out for me as it was difficult to find work in Australia without a working visa. So, I went back to Southern California and began my professional career. I was eager to work and took a job at Clean Energy Fuels. I worked on a team to achieve operational excellence as a Maintenance Planner for their natural gas stations throughout the US. During my time there, I was able to learn about working in a corporate office environment, preventative maintenance, process improvement, and cross-functional collaboration to complete projects on schedule. With not much room to grow in that department and a desire for more exciting work, I decided to pursue a job in geology, my first college degree. I was fortunate to land a job at Southern California Geotechnical. As a Geologist there, I was finally working outside again and with a great group of coworkers (not just the rocks!). I conducted field explorations and lab tests to determine critical soil properties of the land prior to commercial and industrial buildings being developed on said land. It was fun for a while, until I got to the point where I was in the field every single day. I was driving all over Southern California working long, physically demanding hours with little or no time to perform lab tests and produce field reports. It got to the point where I had to prioritize my physical and mental health over this type of work, so I decided to leave and take a bit of a sabbatical. I learned many unique skills when working there, such as coordinating projects & schedules with colleagues & contractors, geotechnical analysis, and time management with multiple projects at once.

When I was ready to get back in the job force, I was asked by a family member to help his friend with a sales opening in Los Angeles. I had no experience in outside sales so I was excited for a new challenge. However, it did not take long to determine that sales was not for me and at the end of my contract, we decided to split ways. As I searched for a new job in Los Angeles, a family friend reached out to me about a Technical Recruiter opening at Apex Systems. With an interest in I.T., I thought of what a great opportunity this could be for me. I interviewed and landed the job! After some training and hands-on experience as a recruiter, I slowly realized how enjoyable and rewarding this career was for me. I learned many valuable skills that can translate into all aspects of life including: communication, organization, problem-solving, leadership, collaboration, and confidence in myself. Everything was going very well as I succeeded in my role and was promoted to a Professional Recruiter, then to Team Lead and finally to Recruiting Manager. Our office had doubled both in size and revenue before the COVID-19 pandemic. Unfortunately, a lot of that changed as the world endured the global pandemic.

This brings us to January 2021, where the staffing industry was still struggling and life was about to change for me. At the start of 2021, I had a minor accident in my apartment that led to a concussion. I do have a history of concussions in the past with several throughout my childhood up until the end of college. So I knew what it felt like and what to expect. However, this one felt different and I did not heal from the injury in the typical one-month time-frame. I was diagnosed with Post Concussion Syndrome (PCS) where the symptoms of a concussion last much longer than expected after injury. I had issues with headaches, dizziness, concentration, memory, blurry vision, fatigue, light & noise sensitivity, and a variety of psychological issues including anxiety, depression, and insomnia. I saw many doctors and went through a lot of therapy to recover. It was a very difficult year, with all the above affecting me and causing me to take a medical leave of absence from work and eventually permanently leave the company. I lost friends, time, and a sense of myself. It took over a year to fully recover and be back to normal. A new normal but I was me again.

When I was physically and mentally ready to work, I decided to ease into the working world again and start part-time. I became a Bank Teller at Wells Fargo because I thought it would be a smooth transition following my recovery. The first few months were challenging, but it felt great to be back at work and feeling normal again. Within six months, I was working full-time at the bank and continued to grow my skills and confidence. I knew that this role was a stepping stone for a bigger and brighter future, and it has helped me tremendously. During my recovery, I had a lot of time to reflect on the important things in life and how precious time on Earth truly is. That is when I realized that I must do something in life that makes me happy and I decided to pursue my true passion in lifeâ€¦DATA! My love for analyzing any information or data to help people and businesses is what drove me towards this field. It's exciting to know that data analysis is such a powerful tool to solve personal and business-related real-world problems. Now, after educating myself in data analytics and getting some hands-on experience through my projects, I am ready for my next chapter in life as a Data Analyst.

Outside of work, I enjoy the outdoors and time with family and friends. Some of my favorite activities include a good hike, camping trip, playing basketball or swimming. During my free time, I like to cook, read, watch movies, do puzzles, and play video games.
